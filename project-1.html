Project 1

Skin Segmentation Project is a Machine learning based project which create and detects skin lesion segment which help in detecting skin cancer.

Code

Project Overview

In this project, I developed a powerful deep learning-based skin segmentation model aimed at assisting in the detection of skin cancer. By leveraging the strengths of two prominent architectures, UNet and ResNet, I created a stacked ensemble model that produces high-fidelity skin segmentation images. This approach showcases the potential of utilizing machine learning and deep learning techniques in the field of medical imaging. The primary objective of the project was to accurately segment the skin region in images, enabling efficient analysis and detection of skin cancer. Skin segmentation plays a crucial role in identifying and isolating skin lesions, which are indicative of potential cancerous areas. To accomplish this, I employed UNet and ResNet, which are well-established deep learning architectures widely recognized for their effectiveness in various image analysis tasks. The stacked ensemble model combines the strengths of both architectures, taking advantage of their unique features and capabilities to achieve improved segmentation results. Throughout the project, extensive experimentation was conducted to fine-tune the model and optimize its performance. The model was trained on a large dataset of skin images, carefully annotated with ground truth skin segmentation masks. This training process allowed the model to learn the intricate patterns and characteristics of healthy and abnormal skin regions.

After training, the model achieved a remarkable accuracy rate of 95% in skin segmentation, demonstrating its robustness and effectiveness. This high accuracy indicates the potential of the proposed approach in aiding medical professionals in the early detection and diagnosis of skin cancer. The developed skin segmentation model holds promise in providing valuable assistance to dermatologists and medical practitioners, enabling them to analyze skin images more efficiently and accurately. By highlighting and isolating suspicious skin regions, the model can help identify potential skin cancer cases, leading to timely intervention and improved patient outcomes. The project contributes to the advancement of medical imaging and the application of machine learning and deep learning techniques in healthcare. The developed skin segmentation model serves as a valuable tool in the fight against skin cancer, offering a non-invasive and automated approach to assist in early detection and diagnosis, ultimately saving lives and improving patient care.
